Marine Corps.

I served in the United States Marine Corps from June 2012 to June 2016, completing two active-duty deployments and earning the rank of Corporal. Throughout my service, I developed a strong foundation in leadership, technical proficiency, and operational planning—skills that continue to influence my professional approach today.

My first deployment was with 1st Battalion, 6th Marine Regiment in 2014 aboard the USS Bataan , an amphibious assault ship operating in the Mediterranean Sea and the Middle East. As an 0331 Machine Gunner and Team Leader, I was responsible for the maintenance and tactical employment of crew-served weapons systems, as well as the training, welfare, and mission-readiness of my team. I routinely operated in high-pressure environments that demanded precise coordination, adaptability, and strategic thinking.

In 2015, I deployed with 1st Battalion, 2nd Marine Regiment to Japan, where we conducted bilateral training exercises with both the Japan Self-Defense Force and the Republic of Korea Marine Corps. During this deployment, I was promoted to the rank of Corporal, recognizing my ability to lead by example and mentor junior Marines. I also served in roles requiring logistical coordination, equipment accountability, and cross-cultural communication—skills that translated directly to project management and systems-based thinking.

I was honorably discharged in June 2016. My time in the Marine Corps instilled in me a results-driven mindset, strong attention to detail, and the ability to lead and execute under pressure—qualities I now apply to every professional challenge I take on.

Education

Robert Morris University

After completing my service in the Marine Corps, I enrolled at Robert Morris University, where I pursued a Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Information Systems with a concentration in Software Development. I began my studies in the Fall of 2016 and graduated in the Spring of 2020.

Throughout my academic journey, I balanced a full-time job during my first two years, developing strong time management skills and a disciplined work ethic. In the summer of 2019, I secured a seasonal role that led to an internship with Meaden & Moore, eventually earning a position as a Staff Consultant. This experience gave me hands-on exposure to business systems, client-facing consulting work, and the application of technical solutions in real-world environments.

My final semester coincided with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, which introduced significant and sudden challenges. Classes shifted to remote learning, career fairs and networking events were canceled, and job prospects became uncertain. Despite the disruption, I remained focused on completing my degree and adapting to a rapidly changing academic and professional landscape. Navigating this period required resilience, flexibility, and a self-driven mindset—qualities I carry with me into every project and professional engagement.

Career

Meaden & Moore

2019-2022

My time at Meaden & Moore began in 2019 while I was still completing my degree at Robert Morris University. I joined the Management Consulting Services division as an intern, where I was introduced to enterprise resource planning (ERP) solutions with a focus on both systems implementation and talent and leadership development. During my internship, I concentrated on understanding standard ERP workflows and had the opportunity to shadow senior managers on client-facing projects involving system analysis, process flow design, and documentation.

As I gained hands-on experience, I began working specifically with Bizowie Cloud ERP—a cloud-based platform—learning its architecture and integration methods. Upon completing my internship, I was offered a full-time role as a Staff Consultant.

In this role, I expanded on my foundational skills and took on greater responsibility. I conducted one-on-one client training sessions across multiple ERP modules, performed on-site discovery and needs assessments, and managed client accounts and relationships. I also developed custom reports and correspondence tools tailored to client requirements, using a combination of HTML5, CSS, JavaScript, and the Templatetool Kit. These tools retrieved and processed data via Bizowie Cloud ERP’s API. Additionally, I created custom scripts and automation solutions to streamline client workflows, using primarily JavaScript and Templatetool Kit.

My time at Meaden & Moore concluded in early 2022, marking the end of a highly formative chapter in my career. The experience sharpened both my technical and interpersonal skills and strengthened my ability to deliver tailored, scalable solutions to clients across a range of industries.

Moonlight N' Stuff

2022 - Current

I joined Moonlight N’Stuff in 2022 as a Lead Software Developer and Project Manager, though my role encompasses much more than the title suggests. Moonlight N’Stuff is a marketing and web development agency founded by Kyle Flynn, composed of a small, agile team with diverse professional backgrounds. As the sole developer and ERP expert on the team, I’ve played a key role in expanding the company’s technical capabilities and service offerings.

While I continue to work with Bizowie Cloud ERP, my current focus has shifted more heavily toward front-end web development and full-cycle project management. One of my most significant contributions has been the design and oversight of a custom in-house ERP system currently in development for a client. I’m managing the entire ERP project, collaborating with a senior full-stack developer who brings over 12 years of experience to the build. I lead all project planning, client communication, and timeline execution—ensuring we meet key milestones consistently. We are on track for a client go-live in January 2026, with plans to publicly launch the platform by late 2026 or early 2027.

In addition to managing this large-scale ERP initiative, I am also responsible for developing all system correspondences and reporting features, utilizing Svelte, JavaScript, TailwindCSS, and standard CSS. My broader front-end development work includes everything from full-site redesigns to building custom web pages, using technologies such as HTML5, CSS, Bootstrap 5, and JavaScript.

Ever Growing

My professional development is a continuous and intentional process. I believe that staying competitive in the tech industry—especially in areas as dynamic as web development and ERP systems—requires constant learning and adaptation. I regularly attend webinars, technical workshops, and virtual conferences to stay current with new tools, best practices, and emerging trends across both the software development and enterprise systems landscapes.

Whether it’s advanced ERP implementation strategies or a refresher on core programming principles, I actively seek out courses at all levels. I take both beginner and advanced training modules to broaden my foundation and strengthen overlooked areas. I’m especially passionate about ERP systems, and I invest time in exploring new features, API capabilities, and workflow improvements for platforms like Bizowie Cloud ERP. I also keep up with ERP-focused forums, documentation releases, and user communities to stay informed and troubleshoot real-world scenarios.

Beyond formal learning, I follow senior developers, ERP architects, and tech thought leaders on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to stay engaged with current practices in system design, scalability, integration, and business process optimization. These insights help me bridge the gap between technical implementation and operational impact—something especially valuable in ERP consulting and development.

I also engage in conversations with other developers and consultants, exchanging ideas and problem-solving strategies via Slack channels, Discord communities, and local meetups. Reading industry blogs, tech journals, and whitepapers is part of my regular routine, as is experimenting with new tools and frameworks in sandbox environments. This hands-on approach helps me stay sharp and assess how new solutions can fit into existing systems or workflows.

In addition, I contribute to internal documentation, mentor team members, and translate complex technical concepts for non-technical stakeholders—especially in ERP-related projects where cross-functional clarity is key. Whether I’m building custom modules, automating business processes, or designing client-facing reports, I treat every project as an opportunity to refine my skills and deliver impactful solutions.

Ultimately, I view my profession as a craft—one that is constantly evolving. My goal is not only to stay current but to become a driving force in both development and ERP implementation through continuous learning, collaboration, and a relentless focus on real-world value.
